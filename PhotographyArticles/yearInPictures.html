A year in 24 pictures

24 pictures. 24 pictures to represent 12 months. That's 365 or 366 days. It's 525601 minutes. That is a lot of time, a lot of possibilities, things that can happen.
Good, bad, middle ground, no ground, possibility is endless, never-ending, everything can happen, everything is chaos.

How do you describe a year in only 24 pictures? Think about it, a 32GB SD card can hold about 945 pictures of full raw format pictures from my camera. Say that you fill a SD card a day for a year, that is 11680GB or what is the same, 11 terabytes of data, or what is more impressive 344925 pictures.
There's a lot that can happen in those three hundred and something thousand pictures, how I wonder again? How do you shorten it down to just 24?

You can't, it's impossible. But you can try your best, make it all be as clear, cohesive and simple as possible. Shorten the chances for error, for missing something, to not think about things that you could have photographed. For what is photography but a way to remember our past, to capture time in a bottle of our own creation for us to look at whenever we want.

Unlike our brains, photographs once taken remain in its initial state forever, there's no chance for false memories or false edits, no chance for error. Once it's taken you can try and fix it through editing, but the moment is gone.
No one picture can ever be the same, much like no two memories can ever be the same. But we can try to evoke those same thoughts that we got when we created an important memory in other people, through photography. To make them feel the warmth of a hug, of a sunset, of a day at the beach, of a longing goodbye that always seems to come at the wrong time.

Through these pictures I want to show that, I want to showcase not only my second year in college (June 2024 to May 2025), but also the feelings that came with it, the good, the bad, the middle ground and the never-ending beauty that comes from how chaotic human lives are.
