How To Be Safe With Fire!

So you want to work with fire, huh? Thats great!!

However there are a couple of considerations you need to have before you do so, mostly so you can keep yourself safe but also so you can keep your friends/helpers safe!

Because that is the first consideration you need to have bring a friend or helper with you if you are a professional, no matter how much control you think you have over it, conditions can change, wind can pick up or change directions at any point in time and blast you in the face with it and guess what? You are very flammable! So bring a friend so they can help put the fire on you out if you need!

The second thing you need to take into consideration is weather conditions!


Say you prepare the best outing with your friends, you have all your equipment and materials the perfect location picked out and when you get to the location it is really windy and the direction of it is a bit unpredictable, should you
A) Keep going with what was scheduled and do the photoshoot
or
B) Cancel and just hangout with your friends without fire at the very least until conditions improve

If for whatever reason your answer was A, then please pay a lot of attention to the rest of this article. The answer is B, just in case, conditions should never be a risk, wind is inevitable when working outside (where you should be if you are working with fire) but if it picks up or is already up then you should not continue shooting, depending on when in the day the shoot is, you are going to be needing to get really close to the fire and it is not worth to have your eyes look like a red raccoon because they got burnt (trust me it is neither pretty nor fun look to have).

The last, but equally as important consideration you need to have is How you are going to turn the fire off in case of an emergency and when you are done with the shoot. Eventually you need to end the shoot and under no circumstances should you leave it on, doesnt matter if you are in the middle of a tundra, thus you need to have some way to turn it off, be it a fire-extinguisher, water or whatever other form of turning off a fire you might think off, something needs to be available in case of an emergency or when it is done.

Personally I like to have a bucket with water on the ready in case of an emergency and be near a large body of water, like a river or a lake, that way if I need to get more water fast I can throw the bucket into the river and run back to the fire, which also explains why you need a friend, sometimes you might get lost in the pictures, your friend can help deal with the emergency if you realise too late or it is getting too out of hand. So yeah I hope this helps you be better ready to work with fire!

Note on materials: I like to work around bonfires, preferably bonfires that were put there by professionals, so not something I just put down in some random place. Specifically I like to use wood to work on them, because to me it is easier to deal with, some paper/cardboard and a lighter is all you need to make it work. However Coal is also a good substitute if there is not enough space for wood or it is too cold for the wood to catch. It is also fun to have other things to burn, like found objects like tennis balls, cardboard or an old T-Shirt, this way you get some more interesting pictures!
Lastly sometimes in the real cold (like Minnesota cold) lighter fluid can help with lighting the wood cause it is cold and wet, HOWEVER, make sure to follow all the things I said before about friends, weather and water, and more importantly be far from the bonfire because it will become more unpredictable, it is best to have someone around that has had experience using lighter fluid to light fires, than not, essentially please be careful around fire
