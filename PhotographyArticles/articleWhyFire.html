Why Fire?

Because it is the most versatile subject to picture, one second you can have a normal looking bonfire and the next you might be staring eye to eye to a creature akin to a phoenix, it all comes down to what the objects that you used to light it are and how and when you click the trigger!

That was an easy answer right? But what is the actual context for it? In what sense is fire a good subject to photograph?

Well that would be in the field of creative photography of course, creative photography can mean a lot of things, it can be something like the one by Visual Wilderness: "... [it] involves integrating additional elements to enhance the mood or message of an image."


Or it can be something like the one by the Nashville Film Institute: "Creative photography is a technique by which you combine different shapes, colors, and forms of ordinary moments and turn them into creative art using your photography skills"


To me on the other hand the definition is both simple and confusing, "Creative photography is the art of a planned photograph"
Planned photograph? What do you mean by that? Good questions! Essentially a planned photograph is something that does not just happen and you picture, it is something that you orchestrated, for example the picture that I use in every header on this website and is next to this text that photograph depicts a paper boat on a surface of water (a lake specifically) with a picture attached to it that is being burned.
This was a planned picture, I had to make the boat, attach the picture and set it ablaze thus this is an example of a creative photograph. It is essentially a picture that makes you stop to look at it and go "How does this even happen?"

Creative photography does not have to be this big thing (like finding the time to go to a lake so you can photograph a boat on fire), it can be something simple, maybe you find a piece of trash that you really like the way that it looks and you think "Huh, I can take a good picture with this"

Another example of simple creative photography is the picture next to this text, it is my very first attempt at creative photography, all it is, is a dark room, the lowest shutter speed my camera can handle (which is 30 seconds), a flashlight and about 17 failed attempts where I tried to write my initials with the light.

To conclude, creative photography is creative because it requires some thought and planning it is not simply pulling out your phone camera and taking a picture of whatever you are looking at, say the sun (that is nature photography and it also requires some planning but in a different way, but you could make it creative with a little editing once the picture was taken), creative photography is meant to be eye-catching to make viewers wonder "How did this came to be?"

A good example of this question is my pictures of fire, how was I able to create a fish and a phoenix out of it? That is for me to know and you to find out! Experiment! Have fun with it! Do it safely if you are going to be working with fire! It is a tricky beast always have water nearby!
