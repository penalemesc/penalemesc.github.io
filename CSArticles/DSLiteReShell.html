DS Lite Mods!

Project Status:
Hardware: Completed
Software: Completed

Hello! Here is an exploration of the 2 mods (or modifications) that I did on my DS Lite! These mods where done following 2 different tutorials by the Youtuber Elliot ColL. He does a lot of repairs, moslty on the things that interest him, be it Nintendo, watches or other equipment!
Because of having followed those specific tutorials, the fact I did not do any modifications of my own to the device other than the ones presented in those tutorials and this project happening in june of '24 which makes it so that I do not remmeber the specifics; rather than the usual tutorial included and exploration, this is more of an exploration of why did I mod my DS Lite, what mods I did, and a basic idea of how and things that could and went wrong. Potentially in the future I will update this site again and include an explanation by me of how to do this process, specially considering the project that I will be working on this week.

So then why did you mod a DS Conrado?
To answer this a little bit of a story, over the summer (North hemisphere based, so June to September) of '24 there was the yearly garage sale at Carleton College called Lighten Up!, in it I found a grey DS Lite for 5 bucks! "What a steal!" is what I thought as at the time the only thing I saw wrong with it was the fact that the left hinge was gone (literally nowhere around), but if you for some reason know the way that DS lites are wired then you will know that this is not really a problem as there is nothing that goes through there this hinge is literally only meant to be hinged on (unlike the right hinge where wires go through it!).
Turns out, I was right, that was the only thing that was wrong with it, not even the bottom screen (this is a foreshadow)! So I thought "What if I find a new shell for it and reshell it?" And it coincided that a couple months before, Elliot Coll (who I was already a fan off) had released a video reshelling a DS Lite with a Pokedex themed shell.

Now I love Pokémon so this was a no brainer to me, I looked for the video again, bought the case and went at it, I followed his tutorial to the T, though I did notice that I had a lot of issue with unlocking the touch screen connector when I was dissasembling the bottom screen, but did not pay it much mind (another foreshadow).
Went through the whole reshelling, putting on the beautiful back cover for the top screen, fitting the top wires through the right hinge, connecting everything, getting confused by the left and right bumpers because they really liked to jump from their positions as I put on the back shell or by the battery nut which loves to move around and get off the shell. Eventually I got it all together and did the final check to see if everything worked, screen's booted and went into menu, buttons all worked and it was time for the final test, touch screen test ... as one can infer from the foreshadowing, it! Did not work!
